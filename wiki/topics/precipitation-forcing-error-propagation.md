# Precipitation Forcing Error Propagation

## Summary

Hydrological outputs do not inherit precipitation error in a simple one-to-one way. In China, forcing errors can be damped, reshaped, or amplified as they move through runoff generation and routing, and the effect depends on product choice and hydroclimate setting.

## Key Points

- Satellite precipitation products vary substantially in error structure, especially between gauge-adjusted and non-adjusted products.
- Routing can reduce the apparent magnitude of forcing error at the streamflow stage relative to the runoff stage.
- Error amplification risk is higher in dry, water-limited, and smaller catchments.
- Forcing-quality assessment should therefore be tied to the downstream hydrological task rather than treated as a standalone ranking problem.

## Evidence Base

- [[sources/2024-satellite-based-precipitation-error-propagation-in-the-hydrological-modeling-chain-across-china]] evaluates ten satellite precipitation products across 366 Chinese catchments and shows that routing often dampens error propagation but not uniformly.
